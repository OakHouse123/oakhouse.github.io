    <h2>Our Approach to Care</h2>

    <p>
      At Oak House Exeter we offer a special resource for those living with dementia or other neurodiverse conditions. We provide a program of positive stimulation within a secure, caring and intimate home setting. Experience has shown that it is not sufficient just to care for people with this condition â€“ if abilities are to be maintained as far as possible then active stimulation of mental function is of the utmost importance.
    </p>


<!-- Uniform Image Row -->
<div style="display: flex;flex-wrap: wrap;justify-content: center;gap: 10px;margin: 30px 0">
  <img src="https://passionate-diannesmyth.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/dsc04187-edited.jpg?w=1366" alt="Oak House Inside" style="width: auto;height: 350px;object-fit: cover;border-radius: 0">
<img src="https://passionate-diannesmyth.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/dsc04182-edited.jpg" alt="Oak House Inside" style="width: auto;height: 350px;object-fit: cover;border-radius: 0">
</div>

  
    <p>
      Our core principle of care is an empathetic, psychosocial approach that mirrors, as far as possible, the environment of the resident's own home. Sufferers from dementia, as part of the process, have an altered view of the perception of reality from others. It is not productive to try to reorient that perception to accord with the normal view or, indeed, to try to suppress it with medication. Either of those approaches ends with frustration for both carer and sufferer.
    </p>

    <p>
      A psychosocial-based programme, on the contrary, is a communicative strategy. Its principle is that it matters little that perceptions differ between carer and sufferer â€” what's important is the establishment of communication that allows both to exist together. Frustration, and the difficulties of behaviour that follow, are therefore minimised. Our staff have a high degree of training for this approach and are committed to our values. Our aim is to ensure that the highest standard of care is provided for each individual's physical, mental and social care needs.
    </p>

    <p>
      At Oak House we have a dedicated activities organiser who engages our residents in both familiar and new interests that encourage both physical and social stimulation. In addition to our in-house activities, we also benefit from visitors who provide services such as fitness and reminiscent sessions, and many other social activities.
    </p>
